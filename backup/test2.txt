Jallianwala Bagh or the garden of the Jallah-man, with its well, implies that it was once green and flowering.[1] Over the years it had become popular as a recreation ground and an area of rest for those visiting the nearby Golden temple.[1] In 1919, it was a dried-out plot, surrounded by tightly packed multi-occupancy buildings divided by some narrow streets, and having only one entrance and exit route.[5] It was unoccupied and surrounded by a wall.[5] The place derives its name from the Jallianwalia family.[6]
