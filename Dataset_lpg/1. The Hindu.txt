1. The Hindu (Analytical & Policy-Focused)
India’s LPG consumption has witnessed steady growth over the past decade, driven largely by government initiatives such as the Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana. The scheme has significantly expanded access to clean cooking fuel in rural households, reducing dependence on biomass.
However, recent data suggests a marginal slowdown in refill rates among low-income beneficiaries due to rising LPG prices. Policymakers are now considering targeted subsidies to ensure sustained usage. The challenge lies not just in access, but in affordability and behavioral transition.
