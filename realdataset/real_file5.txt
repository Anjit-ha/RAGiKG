Greta Thunberg, a Swedish climate activist born in 2003, started the Fridays for Future movement in 2018 when she began striking outside the Swedish Parliament to demand stronger climate action. Fridays for Future inspired millions of students worldwide to participate in climate strikes, with the largest global strike in September 2019 drawing over four million participants across 161 countries. Greta Thunberg addressed the United Nations Climate Action Summit in 2019, where she criticized world leaders for insufficient climate action with her speech titled How Dare You. The Loss and Damage Fund was officially established at COP27 in Sharm el-Sheikh, Egypt in 2022. The Loss and Damage Fund is designed to provide financial support to developing countries that are most vulnerable to climate impacts they did not cause. The World Bank was designated as an interim host of the Loss and Damage Fund. At COP28 in Dubai, developed countries pledged over 700 million dollars to the Loss and Damage Fund, though critics including Greenpeace and Fridays for Future argued this was far less than what was needed. The Climate Vulnerable Forum is a group of 68 countries that are most affected by climate change, including Bangladesh, the Maldives, Ethiopia, and the Philippines. The Maldives, an island nation in the Indian Ocean, faces an existential threat from sea level rise and could become uninhabitable by 2100 according to the IPCC. Bangladesh faces severe flooding, cyclones, and salinity intrusion due to climate change and works closely with the World Bank on climate adaptation financing. The Rockefeller Foundation committed one billion dollars to help developing countries transition away from coal through its Coal to Clean Energy initiative. BlackRock, the world's largest asset manager, announced it would integrate climate risk into its investment decisions and joined the Net Zero Asset Managers initiative alongside Vanguard and Fidelity.
