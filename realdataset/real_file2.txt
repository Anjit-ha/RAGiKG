Tesla, founded by Elon Musk, JB Straubel, Martin Eberhard, Marc Tarpenning, and Ian Wright in 2003, is a major contributor to electric vehicle adoption globally. Tesla produces electric cars, solar panels, and battery storage systems such as the Powerwall and Megapack. Tesla is headquartered in Austin, Texas. The International Energy Agency, or IEA, was founded in 1974 in response to the 1973 oil crisis and is headquartered in Paris, France. The IEA published its landmark Net Zero by 2050 report in 2021, stating that no new oil, gas, or coal development should be approved beyond that year to meet climate targets. The IEA works closely with the IPCC to align energy transition roadmaps with climate science. Carbon capture and storage, or CCS, is a technology that captures carbon dioxide emissions from sources such as power plants and industrial processes and stores it underground. Climeworks, a Swiss company founded in 2009, operates the world's first commercial direct air capture plant in Iceland called Orca, which opened in 2021. Climeworks also partnered with Microsoft to remove carbon dioxide from the atmosphere as part of Microsoft's carbon negative pledge. Microsoft committed to becoming carbon negative by 2030 and to remove all the carbon it has emitted since its founding by 2050. Google achieved carbon neutrality in 2007 and committed to operating on 24/7 carbon-free energy by 2030. Google signed a large renewable energy deal with Ørsted, a Danish energy company that is the world's largest offshore wind developer. Ørsted divested from fossil fuels and transformed into a renewable energy company after the Danish government directed it to do so in 2012.
