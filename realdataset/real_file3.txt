The COP28 United Nations Climate Conference was held in Dubai, United Arab Emirates in November and December 2023. COP28 was presided over by Sultan Al Jaber, who is also the CEO of the Abu Dhabi National Oil Company, ADNOC, which drew criticism from Greenpeace and other environmental organizations. At COP28, more than 130 countries signed a pledge to triple renewable energy capacity by 2030 and double energy efficiency improvements. The European Union adopted the European Green Deal in 2020, which aims to make Europe the first climate-neutral continent by 2050. The European Green Deal was proposed by the European Commission under President Ursula von der Leyen. The EU also introduced the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism, or CBAM, which places a carbon price on imports from countries with weaker climate policies, particularly targeting steel, cement, aluminium, fertilizers, electricity, and hydrogen. China is the world's largest emitter of greenhouse gases, responsible for approximately 30 percent of global CO2 emissions. China pledged at the Paris Agreement to achieve carbon neutrality by 2060 and to peak its carbon emissions before 2030. India is the third largest emitter of greenhouse gases. India committed to achieving net zero emissions by 2070 at COP26 in Glasgow in 2021. The World Bank provides financing to developing countries for climate adaptation and mitigation projects through its Climate Change Action Plan. The World Bank partnered with the IEA and the IPCC to produce joint reports on energy access and climate resilience in sub-Saharan Africa.
