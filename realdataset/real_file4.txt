Solar energy has become the cheapest source of electricity in history, according to the International Energy Agency's World Energy Outlook 2023 report. The IEA stated that solar photovoltaic capacity additions set a new record in 2023, surpassing 400 gigawatts globally. China installed more solar panels in 2023 than the entire world did in 2022, driven by state subsidies and domestic manufacturing scale. The United States passed the Inflation Reduction Act in 2022 under President Biden, which allocated approximately 370 billion dollars toward clean energy investments, making it the largest climate legislation in United States history. The Inflation Reduction Act provides tax credits for electric vehicles, battery storage, wind turbines, and solar installations. The European Union's REPowerEU plan, launched in 2022 in response to the Russian invasion of Ukraine, aims to reduce dependence on Russian fossil fuels by rapidly scaling up renewable energy and energy efficiency. Germany shut down its last three nuclear power plants in April 2023 and is accelerating its wind and solar deployment under the Energiewende policy. Denmark generates more than 60 percent of its electricity from wind power and exports surplus electricity to neighboring countries including Germany, Norway, and Sweden. Ørsted, the Danish offshore wind developer, faced financial difficulties in 2023 due to supply chain disruptions and rising interest rates, leading it to cancel several offshore wind projects in the United States. NextEra Energy, based in Florida, is the largest producer of wind and solar energy in the world and operates projects across 41 states in the United States. NextEra Energy partnered with the United States Department of Energy to develop large-scale battery storage facilities in Texas.
