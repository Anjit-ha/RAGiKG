The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change, known as the IPCC, was established in 1988 by the United Nations Environment Programme and the World Meteorological Organization. The IPCC does not conduct its own research but assesses existing scientific literature to provide policymakers with regular assessments of the scientific basis of climate change, its impacts and future risks, and options for adaptation and mitigation. In 2021, the IPCC released its Sixth Assessment Report, concluding that human influence has warmed the atmosphere, ocean and land unequivocally. The Paris Agreement, adopted in 2015 under the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change, aims to limit global warming to 1.5 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels. The Paris Agreement was signed by 196 countries and entered into force in November 2016. The United States withdrew from the Paris Agreement under President Trump in 2020 but rejoined under President Biden in 2021. Greenpeace is an international non-governmental organization founded in 1971 in Vancouver, Canada. Greenpeace campaigns on climate change, deforestation, overfishing, commercial whaling, genetic engineering, and anti-nuclear issues. Greenpeace supported the Paris Agreement and has lobbied the IPCC to adopt stronger emissions targets. The Natural Resources Defense Council, or NRDC, is an American nonprofit environmental advocacy group founded in 1970. The NRDC has litigated against the United States Environmental Protection Agency to enforce the Clean Air Act to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The Environmental Protection Agency, or EPA, was established in 1970 by President Nixon and is responsible for regulating air and water quality in the United States.
